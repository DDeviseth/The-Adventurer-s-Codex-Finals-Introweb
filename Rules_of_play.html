Rules of Play

Rules of Play

Once your character is created, it's time to learn how to play them in the world of the game. The rules explain how actions, combat, and magic work, but don't worry, at the table, it all feels natural.

Skills

Skills show what your character is good at. When you try something risky, you roll a d20, add the relevant ability modifier, and sometimes your proficiency bonus if you're trained in that skill.

Strength Skills

Athletics (climbing, swimming, grappling)

Dexterity Skills

Acrobatics (balance), Sleight of Hand (tricks, theft), Stealth (hiding)

Intelligence Skills

Arcana (magic lore), History (events), Investigation (clues), Nature (wilderness), Religion (gods)

Wisdom Skills

Animal Handling (control mounts), Insight (read people), Medicine (first aid), Perception (spotting danger), Survival (tracking)

Charisma Skills

Deception (lying), Intimidation (threats), Performance (entertaining), Persuasion (convincing others)

Combat (Step by Step)

Combat is turn-based and organized, but still fast-paced once you learn it. Here's the flow:

Initiative

Everyone rolls a d20 + Dexterity modifier to determine turn order.

Your Turn

On your turn you can move and take one action (attack, cast a spell, dash, dodge, hide, etc.).

Bonus Action

Some classes or abilities let you take an extra quick action.

Reactions

Special actions you can take outside your turn, like making an opportunity attack when an enemy runs past you.

Damage & HP

Attacks that hit deal damage, reducing Hit Points (HP). If you reach 0 HP, you fall unconscious and risk death.

Magic & Spellcasting

Magic adds incredible power and versatility, but it follows rules:

Spell Levels

Ranging from level 0 (cantrips) to level 9 (most powerful).

Spell Slots

Limited uses per day; once used, a slot is spent until you rest.

Casting Requirements

Many spells need verbal (words), somatic (gestures), or material (items) components.

Concentration

Some spells require focus, and you can only concentrate on one spell at a time.

Example Spells:

Fireball (Level 3)

Explodes for 8d6 fire damage in a 20-foot radius.

Cure Wounds (Level 1)

Restores 1d8 + modifier hit points to an ally.

Mage Hand (Cantrip)

Summons a ghostly hand to move objects.

Conditions (Status Effects)

Sometimes characters gain special conditions that affect how they act in battle:

Blinded

You can't see, and attacks against you have advantage.

Charmed

You can't attack the charmer, and they have influence over you.

Frightened

You have disadvantage while the source of fear is in sight.

Paralyzed

You can't move, speak, or act; attackers automatically hit you from close range.

Poisoned

You have disadvantage on attacks and ability checks.

Stunned

You can't move or act, and enemies have advantage against you.
