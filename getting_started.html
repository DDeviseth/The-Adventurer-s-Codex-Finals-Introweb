Getting Started

What Are RPGs?

Roleplaying games (RPGs) are cooperative storytelling adventures where you and your friends take on the roles of heroes in a shared world. Each player creates a character, while one person, called the Game Master (GM) or Dungeon Master in Dungeons & Dragons, guides the story, controls non-player characters, and sets the challenges.

Unlike board games, RPGs don't have a single way to "win." Instead, the goal is to tell stories, solve problems, and experience adventures together. Every game is unique, shaped by your decisions, dice rolls, and creativity.

Dice & How They Work

Most RPGs use special dice to decide the outcome of uncertain actions. These are called polyhedral dice and include: d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, and d100.

d20 (20-sided die): The most important die in Dungeons & Dragons, used for attacks, saving throws, and skill checks.
Damage Dice (d4â€“d12): Weapons and spells roll these to determine how much damage they deal.
Percentile Dice (d100): Rolled for random tables, like magical effects or treasure.

When you attempt something risky, you roll a die and add a bonus from your character's stats. The result is compared to a target number set by the GM, called a Difficulty Class (DC).

Core Mechanics

Once you know the basics of dice, the rest of the game comes down to a few simple rules.

Ability Checks

Roll a d20 + a modifier when trying something difficult, like climbing or sneaking.

Saving Throws

Defensive rolls to avoid danger, such as dodging a fireball or resisting poison.

Attack Rolls

Roll a d20 to see if you hit. If your roll meets or beats the enemy's Armor Class (AC), you deal damage.

Hit Points (HP)

A measure of your health. At 0 HP, your character is unconscious and in danger.

Advantage & Disadvantage

Sometimes you roll twice. With advantage, you take the higher result; with disadvantage, the lower.
