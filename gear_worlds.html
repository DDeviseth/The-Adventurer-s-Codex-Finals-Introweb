Getting Started

Gears and Worlds

Adventurers need more than courage to survive. Weapons, armor, and tools keep you alive in the dungeon, while monsters, treasures, and other worlds make the journey exciting. This chapter introduces the essentials of equipment and the larger universe of roleplaying games.

Weapons

Weapons are the tools of battle. Each has unique properties that affect how it's used:

Heavy

Too large for Small characters to use effectively.

Finesse

Lets you use Dexterity instead of Strength.

Reach

Allows attacks from 10 feet away.

Thrown

Can be thrown as a ranged attack.

Versatile

Can be wielded with one or two hands.

Loading

Slows reload time (like crossbows).

Two-Handed

Requires both hands to use.

Adventuring Gear & Tools

Tools give characters extra abilities both in and out of combat. Proficiency means you can use them effectively.

Thieves' Tools

For picking locks and disabling traps.

Smith's Tools

Repair weapons and armor.

Brewer's Supplies

Create drinks and detect poison.

Alchemist's Supplies

Craft potions or analyze chemicals.

Disguise Kit

Change appearances to blend in.

Forgery Kit

Create false papers or maps.

Monsters & Encounters

The world is full of dangers, from weak goblins to mighty dragons. Each monster has stats—AC, HP, abilities, and attacks—that the Game Master uses to run encounters.

Goblin

Small, sneaky, and quick to ambush.

Orc

Brutish warriors with aggressive tactics.

Zombie

Undead that keep fighting even after taking heavy damage.

Dragon

Legendary creatures with flight, breath weapons, and immense power.

Owlbear

A ferocious beast with claws and beak.

Planes of Existence

Beyond the world you know lies the multiverse:

Material Plane

(the normal world)

Feywild

(magical faerie land)

Shadowfell

(dark reflection)

Elemental Planes

(fire, water, air, earth)

Outer Planes

(heavens, hells, abyss)
