Character Selection

Key Terms & Glossary

Here's a fuller list of terms new players often need:

AC (Armor Class)

The number an attacker must roll to hit you. Higher is better.

HP (Hit Points)

Your health. At 0 HP, you're unconscious and risk death.

DC (Difficulty Class)

The target number you must meet or beat on a roll.

Initiative

Determines turn order in combat. Roll a d20 + Dexterity modifier.

Proficiency Bonus

A bonus added to attacks, skills, or saves you're trained in. Starts at +2.

Saving Throw

A roll to resist harmful effects like spells, traps, or poison.

Ability Check

A roll to attempt an action using one of your six ability scores.

Attack Roll

A d20 roll to see if your attack hits the target.

Critical Hit

A natural 20 on an attack roll, dealing extra damage.

Short Rest

A 1-hour break to recover some health and abilities.

Long Rest

An 8-hour rest that fully restores health and spells.

Conditions

Special effects that hinder you (Blinded, Stunned, Frightened, etc.).

Spell Slot

A resource for casting spells—once used, it's spent until you rest.

Concentration

Focus required to maintain certain spells; broken if you're hurt.

Alignment

A roleplay guideline for morality and behavior (like Lawful Good or Chaotic Evil).

Level Up

When your character gains new abilities by earning experience points (XP).

Beginner Tips

Pick a simple class first

Fighters, rogues, and clerics are great entry points.

Use the character sheet

It tracks your stats, abilities, and inventory, don't keep it all in your head.

Let the story guide you

The rules support the story, not the other way around.

Ask your GM questions

No one knows everything—clarity is part of the fun.

Don't fear failure

A bad roll can lead to the funniest or most memorable moments.

Think like your character

What would they do in this situation?

Keep notes

Writing down NPC names, quests, or treasures helps you stay engaged.

Be a team player

Work with the party, heroes succeed by cooperating.

Stay flexible

Sometimes the GM improvises, sometimes the dice surprise you. Embrace it.

Quick Reference: Common Rolls

Attack: d20 + attack bonus vs. AC.
Skill Check: d20 + ability + proficiency (if trained).
Saving Throw: d20 + ability + proficiency (if proficient).
Damage Roll: Weapon/spell damage die + ability modifier.
Initiative: d20 + Dexterity.

Quick Reference: Common Conditions

Blinded: Can't see, enemies have advantage against you.
Charmed: Friendly to your charmer, harder to resist persuasion.
Frightened: Can't approach the source of fear, disadvantage on rolls.
Paralyzed: Can't move or act, enemies automatically crit in melee.
Poisoned: Disadvantage on attacks and ability checks.
Stunned: Can't move or act, enemies have advantage.
